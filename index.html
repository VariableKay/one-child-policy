The one-child policy isn't awesome at all and in fact it's rather horrendous. Violators would be fined and forced abortions would be carried out. It's atrocious, really.
People would risk losing their jobs for their children. Statistically speaking, this 'family planning' thing would not help China's population at all.

In reality, it would most likely backfire and ruin young couples' dreams of having a big family.
The policy led to sex-selective abortions or infanticide targeting girls, because of a centuries-old social preference for boys.

Luckily for Chinese couples, the one-child policy was lifted on the 1st of January back in 2016.
Unfortunately, there's now a three-child policy. Good luck lol
Other than abortion, if parents did not pay a fine, second children could not be registered in the national household system,
meaning they did not exist legally and so would not have access to social services like health care and education.
